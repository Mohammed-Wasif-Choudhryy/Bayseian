ASSESSMENT DOMAINS 1:
Content, knowledge and mathematical skills (60%)	Strong evidence of an exceptional grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference. Highly appropriate mathematical, statistical and computational techniques have been demonstrated and applied correctly.  	Strong evidence of an excellent grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference.  Appropriate mathematical, statistical and computational techniques have been demonstrated and applied correctly.  	Strong evidence of a very good grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference.  Appropriate mathematical, statistical and computational techniques have been demonstrated and applied correctly.  There are minor doubts, inconsistencies, or not fully justified statements. 	Evidence of a good grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference.  Some appropriate mathematical, statistical and computational techniques have been identified and applied correctly. Some doubts, inconsistencies, or not clearly justified statements.	Evidence of a satisfactory grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference. Little appropriate mathematical, statistical and computational techniques have been identified and applied correctly. There are mathematical errors, inconsistencies, or statements that are not justified.	Very poor evidence of a satisfactory grasp of Bayesian modelling and inference. A very good presentation demonstrating self-direction and understanding of your work. A logical flow from start to finish of the presentation in a good rational way.  A very good explanation of the rationality behind the models, the priors and all model choices. Very good critical evaluation of results and convergence. The level of English and use of language are very good. 	A good presentation demonstrating some self-direction and understanding of your work.
